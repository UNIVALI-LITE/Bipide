Bitwise Shift Operation

In an arithmetic shift, the bits that are shifted out are discarded from each end.

In an arithmetic shift left, zeros are shifted to the right as shown in the picture:

Example:

   00010111 (decimal +23) LEFT-SHIFT
=  00101110 (decimal +46)

In an arithmetic right shift, the sign bit is shifted left, thus preserving the sign of the operand as shown in the picture:

Example:

   10010111 (decimal -105) RIGHT-SHIFT
=  11001011 (decimal -53)
